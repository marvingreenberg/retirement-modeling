## Pre-Retirement Modeling

When **retirement age** is set in the profile settings, the simulation models working years differently from retirement years.

### How It Works

During pre-retirement years (before reaching retirement age):

- **Income covers spending** — Salary and other income streams fund living expenses. No withdrawals are taken from investment accounts for spending.
- **Roth conversions are deferred** — Converting pre-tax accounts to Roth doesn't make sense while employment income fills tax brackets. Conversions begin after retirement when income drops.
- **Tax shortfalls use liquid accounts only** — If taxes owed exceed withholding, the shortfall is covered from cash and brokerage accounts, not from retirement accounts.
- **401k contributions continue** — Pre-tax and Roth 401k contributions from employment income are deposited into retirement accounts each year.
- **Surplus income is invested** — When income exceeds spending, the excess is routed according to the excess income routing setting (brokerage, IRA, or Roth IRA).

### When Retirement Age Is Not Set

If no retirement age is configured, the simulation treats every year as a retirement year — withdrawals and conversions happen from year one. This is the right setting if you're already retired.

### Setting Up Pre-Retirement Scenarios

1. Set your **retirement age** in Profile settings
2. Enter your **current salary** in the Income section (appears automatically when retirement age is set)
3. Configure **401k contributions** if applicable
4. Run the simulation — you'll see salary income in pre-retirement years and withdrawals beginning at retirement
