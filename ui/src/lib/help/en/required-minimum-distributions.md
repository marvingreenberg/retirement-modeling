## Required Minimum Distributions

Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) are mandatory annual withdrawals from pre-tax retirement accounts (Traditional IRA, 401k, 403b, 457b, SEP IRA, SIMPLE IRA). The IRS requires these withdrawals to ensure that tax-deferred funds are eventually taxed as income.

### RMD Age

Under the SECURE 2.0 Act, RMDs begin at age 73 (for those born 1951-1959) and age 75 (for those born 1960 or later). The simulation defaults to age 73 but this can be configured.

RMDs do **not** apply to Roth IRAs during the owner's lifetime. Roth 401k accounts were previously subject to RMDs but this requirement was eliminated starting in 2024.

### How RMDs Work

Each year after reaching RMD age, a minimum amount must be withdrawn from pre-tax accounts. The amount is calculated by dividing the prior year-end balance by a life expectancy factor from IRS Uniform Lifetime Table. The factors decrease with age, resulting in larger required withdrawal percentages over time:

- Age 73: factor 26.5 (≈3.8%)
- Age 80: factor 20.2 (≈5.0%)
- Age 85: factor 16.0 (≈6.3%)
- Age 90: factor 12.2 (≈8.2%)

### Impact on Planning

RMDs can push retirees into higher tax brackets, especially those with large pre-tax balances. This is one reason to consider Roth conversions in early retirement — converting pre-tax funds before RMDs begin reduces the future mandatory withdrawal amounts and their tax impact.

RMD amounts count toward spending needs. If the RMD exceeds the spending target, the excess is routed according to the excess income routing setting (typically to a brokerage account).
