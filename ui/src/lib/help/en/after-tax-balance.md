## After-Tax Estimated Balance

The **After-Tax Est.** adjusts the total portfolio balance to reflect the embedded tax liability of pre-tax accounts. Not all dollars in a retirement portfolio have the same after-tax value:

- **Roth IRA / Roth 401(k)** -- Withdrawals are tax-free. $1 in a Roth is worth $1 after tax.
- **Brokerage** -- Only the _gain_ portion is taxed (at capital gains rates). If your cost basis ratio is 40%, then 60% of each withdrawal is taxable gain. The after-tax value depends on your basis.
- **Traditional IRA / 401(k)** -- Every dollar withdrawn is taxed as ordinary income. $1 in a traditional IRA might only be worth $0.75-$0.85 after federal and state taxes, depending on your bracket.

### How it's calculated

The after-tax estimate discounts pre-tax account balances by the year's effective tax rate:

**After-Tax Est. = Brokerage + Roth + Roth Conversions + Pre-tax x (1 - effective rate)**

This is an approximation -- the actual tax owed depends on how much is withdrawn in a given year and the marginal rates at that time.

### Why it matters

Comparing two scenarios by total balance alone can be misleading. A no-conversion scenario may show a _higher_ total balance, but much of that balance sits in pre-tax accounts with an embedded tax liability. The after-tax estimate reveals that the conversion scenario may actually leave more spendable wealth.

### Inherited assets

For beneficiaries, the tax picture changes:

- **Brokerage accounts** receive a **step-up in cost basis** at death, eliminating capital gains on appreciation during the original owner's lifetime. Only gains _after_ inheritance are taxable.
- **Roth accounts** pass to heirs tax-free (subject to the 10-year distribution rule for non-spouse beneficiaries).
- **Traditional IRA / 401(k)** balances are still taxed as ordinary income when the beneficiary withdraws -- there is no step-up for pre-tax retirement accounts.

This means the after-tax advantage of Roth conversions extends to heirs, while the embedded tax liability of traditional accounts is inherited along with the balance.
